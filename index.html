OUR STORY

Our team has drive, experience and knowledge

We believe notarization should be as modern and accessible as the digital world we live in. Our journey began with the simple question: why should anyone have to travel, wait in line, and deal with cumbersome paperwork just to notarize a document? With extensive backgrounds in technology, compliance, and blockchain, our team set out to build a notary service that is secure, transparent, and available anytime, anywhere.

By leveraging the power of blockchain, we created a platform that’s tamper-proof, efficient, and incredibly user-friendly. With recent changes in notarization laws allowing remote notarization, we knew it was the perfect time to make this vision a reality. Today, DocChain enables individuals and businesses alike to notarize and verify documents online with confidence—saving time, reducing costs, and setting a new standard for digital trust. Our mission is to simplify notarization and make it accessible to everyone, regardless of where they are.

Our vision is to redefine notarization for the digital age—making it secure, accessible, and seamlessly integrated into everyday life. We believe in a future where anyone can notarize important documents from anywhere in the world with just a few clicks, confident in the integrity and transparency that blockchain technology provides. By eliminating the barriers of traditional notary processes, we aim to empower individuals, businesses, and communities with tools that make legal documentation simpler, faster, and more trustworthy. Our goal is to set a new global standard for notarization, where convenience meets security in the most efficient way possible.

DocChain Digital Notary was founded with a clear purpose: to modernize the outdated notarization process and make it accessible to everyone, everywhere. Our journey began as a response to the frustrations we saw in traditional notary services—the need for in-person visits, stacks of paperwork, and high costs. BY CHRISTOPHER ROBISON

California Authorizes Remote Online Notarization

On January 1, 2024, California officially legalized Remote Online Notarization (RON), joining the ranks of 44 other U.S. states that have already adopted the practice. This legislation permits Californians to complete notarizations digitally, without the need for in-person appointments, signaling a shift toward greater convenience and accessibility in the notary sector. California's adoption of RON is expected to set a precedent for other states, enhancing the modernization and efficiency of legal document processes nationwide.
